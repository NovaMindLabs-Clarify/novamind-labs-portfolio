Chef's Notes: The Autumn Menu

October 2, 2026 · Kitchen Team

Every menu change starts the same way: a walk through what the farm delivery actually looks like that week, not what we planned to cook a month ago. This week it was squash, late-season tomatoes, and the first good batch of chanterelles.

The squash soup that opened this piece isn't complicated — brown butter, toasted seeds, a little sage — but it only works because the squash itself is good enough to not need much done to it. That's most of what "seasonal" actually means in this kitchen: less intervention, not more technique.

The short rib stays on a little longer this month, braised slower now that the dining room runs cooler in the evenings. If you're deciding between that and the ribeye, the short rib is the better order on a weeknight — it's built for a slower evening.
